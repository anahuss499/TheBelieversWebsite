‚ú® Legacy & Lessons

Principles of Good Governance: The Khulafa Rashidun demonstrated that Islamic leadership must be based on Shura (consultation), justice, accountability, and adherence to the Qur'an and Sunnah.

Rapid Expansion: Within 30 years, the Islamic state grew from a small community in Arabia to encompassing the Middle East, North Africa, and parts of Central Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

Administrative Innovation: They established systems of governance, legal frameworks, and military organization that became models for future Islamic states.

Personal Integrity: All four Caliphs were known for their personal piety, simple living despite their power, and dedication to Allah Almighty's cause rather than personal gain.
