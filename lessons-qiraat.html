üåü The Three Additional Qira'at

In addition to the seven canonical Qira'at, there are three more authentic recitations that are also mutawatir (mass-transmitted):

  • Abu Ja'far al-Madani[may Allah Almighty have mercy on him] - From Madinah (transmitted by Ibn Wardan & Ibn Jammaz)
  • Ya'qub al-Hadrami[may Allah Almighty have mercy on him] - From Basra (transmitted by Ruways & Rawh)
  • Khalaf[may Allah Almighty have mercy on him] - From Baghdad (transmitted by Ishaq & Idris)

These bring the total to 10 authentic Qira'at, all of which are accepted by the Muslim Ummah.

üìö Understanding the Differences

Types of Variations:

  • Pronunciation differences: Such as whether to say "ŸÖŸéÿßŸÑŸêŸÉ" (Maalik) or "ŸÖŸéŸÑŸêŸÉ" (Malik) in Surah Al-Fatihah
  • Vowel marks: Different diacritical marks on the same letters
  • Letter variations: Minor differences in certain letters that don't change core meanings
  • Pause and continuation: Different rules for where to stop and continue

Important: All variations are divinely preserved and do not contradict each other. They complement one another and enrich the meanings and understanding of the Qur'an.

‚ú® Which Qira'ah Should You Learn?

Hafs 'an Asim is the most widespread recitation today and is used in:

  • Most printed Qur'ans worldwide
  • The majority of Muslim countries
  • Most Qur'an memorization schools (Hifz programs)
  • Online Qur'an platforms and apps

Warsh 'an Nafi' is predominantly used in North and West Africa.

All Qira'at are authentic and acceptable. Learning any of them will help you recite the Qur'an correctly and earn the reward from Allah Almighty.
