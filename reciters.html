Reciter Biographies

Learn more about the voices featured on our Qur'an player.

Muhammad Siddiq al-Minshawi

Murattal · Clear, steady delivery

Al-Minshawi (1920–1969) was an Egyptian Qur'an reciter celebrated for his calm, deeply reflective style and impeccable tajweed. His recordings remain a go-to for learners because every letter is articulated with clarity and humility.

  • Origin: Sohag, Egypt
  • Known for: Crystal-clear makharij and measured pacing ideal for study
  • Legacy: Part of the "Four Great Egyptian Reciters" whose voices shaped modern tilawah

Abdul Basit Abdus Samad

Murattal · Majestic, resonant tone

Shaykh Abdul Basit (1927–1988) captivated listeners with powerful breath control and emotive recitation that popularized Qur'an audio worldwide. His voice is often described as both commanding and deeply soothing.

  • Origin: Armant, Upper Egypt
  • Known for: Effortless long breaths and soaring maqamat transitions
  • Legacy: First major reciter to tour globally and record widely-distributed cassettes
